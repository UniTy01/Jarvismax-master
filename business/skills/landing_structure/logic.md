# Landing Page Structure — Reasoning Structure

## Step 1: Conversion Goal Clarity
- Define the single primary action (signup, buy, demo, waitlist)
- Define secondary actions (learn more, share, bookmark)
- Determine the awareness level of typical visitors
- Map the emotional journey: confused → curious → convinced → committed

## Step 2: Hero Section Design
- Headline: outcome-focused, under 10 words
- Subheadline: explains mechanism in 1-2 sentences
- Primary CTA: high-contrast, action-verb button
- Visual: product screenshot, demo GIF, or hero illustration
- Above the fold: must communicate WHAT + WHY + HOW TO START

## Step 3: Page Section Flow (AIDA)
Design sections in this order:
1. **Hero** — Attention: headline + CTA + visual
2. **Problem** — Interest: name the pain clearly (before state)
3. **Solution** — Desire: show the transformation (after state)
4. **How It Works** — Desire: 3-step process, reduce complexity
5. **Features/Benefits** — Desire: 3-6 key benefits with icons
6. **Social Proof** — Trust: testimonials, logos, stats
7. **Objection Handler** — Trust: FAQ or comparison table
8. **Pricing** — Action: clear tiers if applicable
9. **Final CTA** — Action: repeat the primary CTA with urgency
10. **Footer** — Trust: links, legal, additional resources

## Step 4: Social Proof Placement
- After hero: logo bar (authority)
- After features: testimonials (evidence)
- Before pricing: stats (scale)
- Near CTA: trust badges (safety)

## Step 5: CTA Progression
- Hero CTA: direct action ("Start Free Trial")
- Mid-page CTA: lower commitment ("See How It Works")
- After social proof: social CTA ("Join 10,000+ Users")
- Final CTA: urgency ("Start Now — Free for 14 Days")

## Step 6: Objection Handling Flow
- Address biggest objection BEFORE it becomes a blocker
- Use FAQ format for multiple small objections
- Use comparison table for "why not competitor?"
- Place guarantees near the final CTA
