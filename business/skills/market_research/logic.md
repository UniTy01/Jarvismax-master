# Market Research — Reasoning Structure

## Step 1: Market Definition
- Define the exact market boundaries
- Identify adjacent markets and overlaps
- Establish geographic and demographic scope

## Step 2: Market Sizing (TAM → SAM → SOM)
- **TAM**: Total global revenue if 100% of potential customers adopted
- **SAM**: Portion addressable with current product/channel constraints
- **SOM**: Realistic capture in first 12-24 months given competition and resources
- All estimates must include methodology and sources

## Step 3: Problem Identification
- List top 5 problems experienced by target customers
- Score each by: frequency, severity, willingness-to-pay
- Identify which problems are underserved

## Step 4: Opportunity Scoring
- For each opportunity, score 1-10 on: market size, urgency, competition gap, feasibility
- Justify each score with evidence
- Rank by composite score

## Step 5: Trend Detection
- Identify 3-5 active trends (technology, regulation, behavior)
- Classify as: accelerating, stable, decelerating
- Assess impact timeline (immediate, 6-12mo, 12-24mo)

## Step 6: Risk Assessment
- Identify risks: regulatory, competitive, technological, market
- Score by likelihood (low/med/high) and impact (low/med/high)
- Flag any deal-breakers
