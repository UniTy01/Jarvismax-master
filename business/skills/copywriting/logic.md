# Copywriting — Reasoning Structure

## Step 1: Audience Understanding
- Define who reads this copy (role, pain, desire)
- Identify their language level and vocabulary
- Determine awareness level: unaware → problem-aware → solution-aware → product-aware
- Match tone to audience expectations

## Step 2: Core Message Extraction
- What is the ONE thing the reader must understand?
- What transformation does the product enable? (before → after)
- What is the emotional hook? (fear, aspiration, frustration, curiosity)
- What proof backs the claim?

## Step 3: Headline Generation (5 angles)
- **Benefit-driven**: Lead with the outcome ("Get X without Y")
- **Curiosity**: Create an information gap ("The #1 mistake...")
- **Social proof**: Leverage authority ("Used by 10,000+ teams")
- **Urgency**: Create time pressure ("Before your competitors do")
- **Question**: Engage with a relatable question ("Tired of X?")
- Each headline must be under 12 words
- Each headline must be specific to THIS product

## Step 4: Value Proposition
- Headline: 6-10 words, outcome-focused
- Subheadline: 15-25 words, explains the mechanism
- 3 supporting points: each with icon-worthy clarity
- Must pass the "so what?" test

## Step 5: Calls to Action
- Primary CTA: action verb + benefit ("Start growing today")
- Low-commitment CTA: reduce friction ("See how it works")
- Urgency CTA: time-bound ("Claim your free trial")
- Social CTA: community-based ("Join 5,000+ users")
- Personal CTA: identity-based ("Yes, I want better results")

## Step 6: Objection Handling
- List top 5 objections the audience will have
- For each: acknowledge → reframe → resolve
- Use proof where possible (data, testimonials, guarantees)

## Step 7: Email Subject Lines
- Cold outreach: curiosity + relevance
- Follow-up: value + personalization
- Nurture: education + benefit
- Conversion: urgency + proof
- Re-engagement: question + missed value
