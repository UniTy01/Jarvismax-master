# Product Positioning — Quality Criteria

## Completeness
- Category defined with rationale
- Target customer profile with qualifying AND disqualifying criteria
- At least 3 competitive alternatives including "do nothing"
- At least 2 unique attributes with defensibility assessment
- 2-3 value themes mapping attributes to customer needs
- Positioning statement follows the template format

## Structure
- Positioning statement is one sentence
- Unique attributes are truly unique (not shared with competitors)
- Value themes use customer language, not product jargon

## Coherence
- Positioning statement ties together target, category, benefit, and unique attribute
- Value themes connect logically to the unique attributes
- Target customer matches the market where unique attributes matter most
- Category choice aligns with customer awareness level

## Red Flags
- Positioning statement could be said by any competitor
- "Unique" attributes are actually table stakes
- Category is too broad or too obscure
- Target customer too wide ("everyone") or too narrow (no market)
