# Strategy Reasoning — Quality Criteria

## Completeness
- Situation analysis covers current state, key factors, and unknowns
- At least 3 genuinely different options generated (not variations of one idea)
- Each option has pros, cons, effort, impact, and timeline
- Trade-offs explicitly analyzed across multiple dimensions
- Recommendation includes rationale, confidence, and revisit conditions
- At least 2 risks with mitigation strategies
- Next steps are specific and time-bound

## Structure
- All outputs in correct JSON format
- Options are comparable (same structure)
- Scores are consistent and defensible
- Next steps are actionable (not vague)

## Coherence
- Recommendation follows logically from trade-off analysis
- Risks relate to the recommended option specifically
- Next steps relate to the recommendation
- Confidence level matches the uncertainty in the situation

## Red Flags
- All options are essentially the same approach
- Recommendation contradicts the trade-off analysis
- No consideration of resource constraints
- Generic risks not tied to the specific situation
- Next steps are vague ("do more research")
