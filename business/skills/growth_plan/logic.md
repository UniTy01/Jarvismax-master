# Growth Plan — Reasoning Structure

## Step 1: Growth Engine Selection
- Identify the natural growth engine for this product:
  - **Viral**: product gets better with more users (network effects)
  - **Paid**: unit economics support profitable acquisition
  - **Content/SEO**: product solves searchable problems
  - **Sales**: high ACV justifies direct outreach
  - **Product-led**: free tier drives self-serve adoption
- Most products have a primary + secondary engine
- Choose based on: product type, ACV, market, and team strength

## Step 2: Channel Identification
- List 5-7 potential acquisition channels
- For each: estimate effort, cost, timeline, expected volume
- Score each by: channel-market fit, scalability, defensibility
- Rank top 3-5 by expected ROI
- Include at least one organic and one paid channel

## Step 3: Retention Strategy
- Define activation milestones (what makes a user "activated"?)
- Identify the "aha moment" — when the user first sees value
- Design onboarding to reach the aha moment fastest
- Build retention loops: notifications, content, community, habit triggers
- Define churn signals and intervention strategies

## Step 4: Metrics Framework
- **North star metric**: single metric that captures delivered value
- **Input metrics**: leading indicators you can influence
- **Output metrics**: lagging indicators that measure success
- **Health metrics**: guardrails (churn, NPS, support tickets)
- Each metric must be measurable and have a target

## Step 5: Experiment Roadmap
- Generate 5+ growth experiments
- Score each by ICE: Impact (1-10), Confidence (1-10), Ease (1-10)
- Rank by composite ICE score
- Each experiment: hypothesis, metric, success criteria, timeline
- Include mix of acquisition + activation + retention experiments

## Step 6: 12-Week Timeline
- Weeks 1-2: Foundation (tracking, baseline metrics)
- Weeks 3-4: Quick wins (highest ICE experiments)
- Weeks 5-8: Channel testing (top 2-3 channels)
- Weeks 9-10: Double down (scale what works)
- Weeks 11-12: Review, learn, plan next quarter
